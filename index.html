North India

Taj Mahal, Agra

The epitome of love, this white marble mausoleum is one of the most recognized structures in the world.

Varanasi

One of the oldest cities in the world, Varanasi is a spiritual hub on the banks of the Ganges River.

Leh-Ladakh

Known for its stunning landscapes, monasteries, and the culture of the Tibetan people.

Delhi

The capital city, rich in history and culture, home to landmarks like the Red Fort and India Gate.

Amritsar

Home to the Golden Temple, a spiritual and cultural center for the Sikh religion.

Manali

A popular hill station in the Himalayas, known for its adventure sports and scenic beauty.

Shimla

The capital of Himachal Pradesh, famous for its colonial architecture and pleasant weather.

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park, renowned for its tiger population and diverse wildlife.

Udaipur

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is famous for its palaces, lakes, and romantic settings.

Kashmir

Often referred to as 'Paradise on Earth', Kashmir is known for its beautiful valleys and houseboats on Dal Lake.

South India

Kerala

Known as 'God's Own Country,' Kerala is famous for its backwaters, beaches, and lush greenery.

Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi is an ancient city with ruins dating back to the Vijayanagara Empire.

Mysore

Famous for its royal heritage and the grand Mysore Palace.

Madurai

Home to the magnificent Meenakshi Temple, known for its intricate architecture.

Ooty

A charming hill station in Tamil Nadu, known for its tea plantations and the Nilgiri Mountain Railway.

Pondicherry

A former French colony, Pondicherry is famous for its serene beaches and colonial architecture.

Alleppey

Known for its backwaters, houseboats, and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race.

Coorg

Also known as Kodagu, Coorg is famous for its coffee plantations and scenic landscapes.

Rameswaram

One of the holiest places in India, Rameswaram is known for the Ramanathaswamy Temple and Pamban Bridge.

Mahabalipuram

Famous for its rock-cut temples and monuments, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

East India

Kolkata

The cultural capital of India, known for its colonial architecture, art galleries, and vibrant festivals.

Darjeeling

Famous for its tea plantations and stunning views of the Himalayas.

Konark

Home to the Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its stunning architecture.

Sundarbans

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its mangrove forests and the Royal Bengal Tiger.

Guwahati

The gateway to the Northeast, Guwahati is known for the Kamakhya Temple and Brahmaputra River.

Bhubaneswar

Known for its ancient temples and rich cultural heritage.

Puri

Famous for its beaches and the Jagannath Temple, an important pilgrimage site.

Ranchi

Known for its waterfalls and the beautiful surroundings of Jharkhand.

Kolkata Book Fair

A major cultural event in Kolkata, showcasing literature and arts.

Sikkim

A beautiful northeastern state known for its mountains and vibrant culture.

West India

Mumbai

The financial capital of India, known for Bollywood and vibrant nightlife.

Goa

Famous for its beautiful beaches, nightlife, and Portuguese heritage.

Udaipur

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is famous for its palaces and romantic settings.

Jaisalmer

Known as the Golden City, famous for its stunning fort and desert landscapes.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves

UNESCO World Heritage Sites known for ancient rock-cut temples and beautiful frescoes.

Mahabaleshwar

A popular hill station in Maharashtra known for its strawberries and scenic views.

Rann of Kutch

A salt desert famous for its white landscape and cultural festivals.

Sabarmati Ashram

The residence of Mahatma Gandhi, an important historical site in Ahmedabad.

Surat

Famous for its textile industry and vibrant food culture.

Lonavala

A hill station known for its beautiful valleys and climate.
